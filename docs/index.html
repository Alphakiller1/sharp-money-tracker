Methodology

Pitcher regression blends the DIPS skill estimate (FIP/xFIP) with balls-in-play luck (BABIP vs .295), sequencing luck (LOB% vs 72%), HR-suppression (xFIP−FIP), strength of schedule faced (OOR), recent K%/BB% trend, and pitch-count fatigue into a single luck score (expected ERA change). Prop leans follow the verdict: a regressing arm projects fewer Ks/outs and more earned runs. Market leans combine both starters' reg/prog with bullpen fatigue/quality, opponent OSI, and splits. Sharp signals de-vig each book and surface only FDR-surviving, profit-positive, CLV-validated segments as tradeable.
