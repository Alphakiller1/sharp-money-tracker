Methodology

Pitcher regression (base) blends the DIPS skill estimate (FIP/xFIP) with balls-in-play luck (BABIP vs .295), sequencing luck (LOB% vs 72%), HR-suppression (xFIP−FIP), strength of schedule faced (OOR), recent K%/BB% trend, and pitch-count fatigue into a single luck score (expected ERA change).

Today's matchup overlay (starters only) adjusts that luck score using the actual slate context from the MLBMA pipeline: projected lineup platoon mix from Today_Lineups (how many LHH/RHH the SP faces, switch hitters bat opposite), split-adjusted opponent OSI and team wRC+ vs hand, the pitcher's vs-LHH / vs-RHH FIP weighted to that mix, ballpark factor at the home stadium, and weather (wind, temperature, dome). Positive matchup adjustment pushes a lucky arm toward regression faster; a cold lineup or pitcher-friendly park can dampen it.

Prop leans follow the matchup-adjusted verdict: a regressing arm in a platoon-stress spot projects fewer Ks/outs and more earned runs. Market leans combine both starters' reg/prog with bullpen fatigue/quality, opponent OSI, and splits. Sharp signals de-vig each book and surface only FDR-surviving, profit-positive, CLV-validated segments as tradeable.
