The quant logic, in plain terms

ROI at entry, not the close. The closing line is near-efficient, so beating it by a point isn't profit. We measure return at the open — the price you can actually act on before sharp money moves it.

CLV (closing line value). The single best-validated predictor of long-run betting profit. If the line moves your way after you bet, you got a good number; we track it on every segment.

Kelly criterion (Thorp/Kelly). Translates edge into growth-optimal stake. A tiny edge implies a tiny Kelly and negligible compounding — which is exactly how we filter out "minute" edges that don't win meaningfully.

Bootstrap confidence interval. Sports payoffs are skewed, so we resample (non-parametric) thousands of times to bound the true ROI. We trust a segment only when its lower bound clears the hurdle.

False-discovery control (Benjamini-Hochberg). We scan many segments, so some look good by luck. BH correction keeps the expected false-positive rate low — no cherry-picking the one lucky split.

Honest-empty. If nothing clears profit + robustness + FDR, the engine reports nothing rather than invent an edge. Beating an efficient market is rare and must be earned by the data.
