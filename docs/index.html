Methodology

Pitcher regression (base) blends the DIPS skill estimate (FIP/xFIP) with balls-in-play luck (BABIP vs .295), sequencing luck (LOB% vs 72%), HR-suppression (xFIP−FIP), strength of schedule faced (OOR), recent K%/BB% trend, and pitch-count fatigue into a single luck score (expected ERA change).

Today's matchup overlay (starters only) adjusts that luck score using the actual slate context from the MLBMA pipeline: projected lineup platoon mix from Today_Lineups (how many LHH/RHH the SP faces, switch hitters bat opposite), split-adjusted opponent OSI and team wRC+ vs hand, the pitcher's vs-LHH / vs-RHH FIP weighted to that mix, Statcast pitch-mix overlap (arsenal usage × whiff/CSW/xwOBA vs opponent team batting), ballpark factor at the home stadium, and weather (wind, temperature, dome). Positive matchup adjustment pushes a lucky arm toward regression faster; a cold lineup or pitcher-friendly park can dampen it.

Prop leans use channel-separated layers (anti double-count): K/BB from L14 rate blend, gamelog K%/BB% trends, Statcast pitch-mix (season or L14), lineup batter pitch-mix, Savant team discipline, and signals conviction (K% vs OBR / BB% vs ABQ). ER/F5 from lineup-weighted projOSI, OSI-tier split FIP, PitchScore anchors, rolling opponent OSI, L10 vs SP-hand cross-check, park/weather, and bullpen quick-hook on outs. Platoon and split FIP adjust the luck stack only — not re-applied on K%/BB% props.

Market leans combine both starters' reg/prog with bullpen fatigue/quality and split context. Supabase projection tracking snapshots every slate for MAE / lean-hit-rate reporting after reconcile. Sharp signals de-vig each book and surface only FDR-surviving segments as tradeable.
